FIT3179: Data Visualization 2

This page displays various visualizations regarding the COVID-19 outbreak during 2020.

COVID-19 Bar Chart:

From the dataset provided, the countries with the greatest number of total cases within the period from 22nd of January, 2020 to 27th of July, 2020 were the United States, Brazil and India. On its own, these numbers seem incredibly high, but can be misleading, as it is important to normalize the data via population, as shown below in the Choropleth map.

COVID-19 Choropleth Map:

After calculating the total number of cases per 10,000 population, we now see a clearler picture in regard to the countries which were disproportionately affected. Countries such as Brazil, Peru, the United States and Oman are among the greatest affected in terms of population. In comparison to this, a country such as India which ranked 3rd in the world for total confirmed cases during this period only had 14.66 cases per 10,000 population, therefore showing the much smaller effect on their total population.

COVID-19 Line Chart:

The line chart depicts the increasing rate of COVID-19 cases experienced throughout the time period of 22nd of January 2020 through to the 27th of July 2020. As can be clearly seen, the number of daily confirmed cases increases dramatically during the first three to four months of the year, stagnates between May and June, and then rises dramatically until it reaches a peak of daily confirmed cases of 282,756 on the 23rd of July 2020.
